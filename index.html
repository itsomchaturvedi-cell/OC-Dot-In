01 About Me

I'm an entrepreneur from Mumbai who has been building and scaling businesses since my teenage years — starting with small, imperfect experiments in digital marketing and e-commerce long before any of them worked. Those early failures taught me more about running a business than any classroom did.

I studied Commerce at NMIMS and Applied Economics at St. Xavier's College, which gave me the analytical grounding I now use to think about markets, operations, and how to build things that actually last.

In 2018, I founded Pepplo Softwares, an IT services and digital marketing agency that grew into a team of 30+ professionals serving clients across India, the US, and Italy. That experience in shipping software and managing operations at scale is the foundation I still build on today.

Today, most of my energy goes into Chartered Studies, an EdTech platform I co-founded to make professional education — for CA, CS, and CMA students — affordable without compromising on quality.

I've also written two books along the way — Blogging: Your Way to Success and a guide to C programming — because writing things down has always been how I think.

03 My Vision

I believe access shouldn't be the reason someone doesn't get a good education, a good product, or a good opportunity.

Every business I build starts from the same question: how do we deliver something genuinely high quality at a price that doesn't exclude the people who need it most? That's the thinking behind Chartered Studies — professional education for CA, CS, and CMA students that competes on quality, not just price, in a market that has historically priced most students out.

Long term, I want to build institutions the way Ratan Tata built his — on trust, integrity, and a genuine commitment to the people they serve — while staying as ambitious and fast-moving as the founders I admire most, like Mark Zuckerberg. Age, background, and starting small have never struck me as good reasons to wait.
